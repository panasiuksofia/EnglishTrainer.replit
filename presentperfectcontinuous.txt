Tense: Present Perfect Continuous

Usage:
1. To emphasize the duration of an action that started in the past and is still continuing now.
   Example: She has been studying for three hours.

2. To express a recent activity that has a visible result in the present.
   Example: You’re out of breath. Have you been running?

3. To describe repeated actions or habits that started in the past and continue to the present.
   Example: I’ve been going to the gym regularly.

Form:
- Affirmative: Subject + have/has + been + verb-ing
  Example: We have been working all day. / He has been reading.

- Negative: Subject + have/has not + been + verb-ing
  Example: She hasn’t been sleeping well.

- Interrogative: Have/Has + subject + been + verb-ing?
  Example: Have they been waiting long? / Has he been exercising?

Time Expressions:
- for, since, lately, recently, all day, all week, how long
