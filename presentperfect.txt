Tense: Present Perfect

Usage:
1. To describe actions that happened at an unspecified time in the past but are relevant to the present.
   Example: I have lost my keys. (I still don't have them)

2. To describe experiences.
   Example: She has visited Paris three times.

3. To describe recent actions with a result in the present.
   Example: He has just finished his homework.

4. To describe actions that started in the past and continue to the present (often with “for” or “since”).
   Example: We have lived here for five years.

Form:
- Affirmative: Subject + have/has + past participle
  Example: I have seen that movie. / She has finished her work.

- Negative: Subject + have/has not + past participle
  Example: They haven’t arrived yet. / He hasn’t called me.

- Interrogative: Have/Has + subject + past participle?
  Example: Have you eaten? / Has she met him?

Past Participle:
- Regular verbs: verb + -ed (worked, played)
- Irregular verbs: unique forms (gone, done, been, eaten, etc.)

Time Expressions:
- already, yet, just, ever, never, recently, so far, since, for
